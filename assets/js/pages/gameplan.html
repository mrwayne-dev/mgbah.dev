01 The System

Same system. Different personnel.

On the team-sheet we are 4-4-2. On the ball we become 3-2-5. The transformation runs through two moves, just like always, one back-line player tucks to make a back 3, and one full-back pushes high into the front 5. The tactic doesn't change for any opponent. Personnel does.

CHANGE 01
Mill is out. Etienne starts RB.
Etienne takes Mill's role. In possession he tucks to become the right of the back 3. Out of possession he is our right-back. Same job, different player.
CHANGE 02
Kennedy starts LCB.
Kennedy comes into the back line as left centre-back. He stays as LCB in both shapes.
CHANGE 03
Goody still pushes high.
Same as always, Goody pushes from LB into the front 5 in possession. He is our left-side weapon and the 2v1 partner with Bobby against their weakest flank.

04 Opposition

Cyper Reapers. Beatable.

They beat CS Royale 6-2. They press high, hold a high line, and play through ball-playing midfielders. That sounds dangerous, until you read what those things create for us. Every strength of theirs is also an opening.

What makes them good
Read it. Respect it. Don't fear it.
  • Calm on the ball, patient possession built around ball-playing midfielders
  • High press is organised and aggressive
  • Suffocated CS Royale in their own half for long periods
  • 6-2 win means they have genuine attacking quality
  • Dangerous left winger plays like an inside 10
What makes them beatable
Their weaknesses are inside their strengths.
  • Their result against another team is not their result against us.
  • Ball-playing midfielders pressing, they are not natural ball-winners. When they commit to pressing, the space behind them is bigger.
  • High line = space in behind. The higher the line, the more exposed they are.
  • Right side is weakest. This is where Bobby and Goody live all game.
  • Counter vulnerability. Pressing high + high line + turnover = goal chance. CS Royale couldn't exploit this. We will.
05 The Three Phases

Bait. Release. Attack.

Our entire attacking idea against this team fits into three words. Everything else, the patterns, the rotations, the rules, serves these three phases.

Phase 01

Bait

Draw their press. Invite them to commit. Make the space appear. Gabi holds the ball deep. We are not rushing. We are setting the trap.

Phase 02

Release

The moment their presser commits and the space opens, we play through. Ibiso receives between the lines. Fast, direct, forward. One pass bypasses their entire press.

Phase 03

Attack

The transition is the chance. Three runners going, full speed, before their defenders recover. This is what we call an "artificial transition", we manufactured it, we finish it.

06 Build-Up & The Bait

Ibiso comes deep. Reuben stays.

The single most important detail of our build-up against this team. Ibiso is the one who drops to collect, not Reuben. Ibiso has the carry and vision to connect the back line to the front 5. Reuben holds his spot as the safety screen.

Build-Up Shape · Baiting The Press
Destiny → Gabi (holds) → Ibiso (drops, receives) → release into half-spaces or over the top.
Us Their press Pass / run
DETAIL 01
Why Ibiso, not Reuben.
Ibiso has the carry and the vision. Reuben is the screen. If Ibiso is also closed, Reuben becomes the simple sideways reset. They cannot press both at once.
DETAIL 02
Why Goody pushes so high.
He stretches the pitch vertically. Pulls their right back deeper, or forces them to ignore him, either way, the wide channel opens. He is also the long-diagonal option for Gabi.
DETAIL 03
Gabi has four options.
Long to Goody. Ground into Ibiso. Direct into Fortune or Rex. Reset to GK or Reuben. His patience creates the options. Do not rush.
07 Their Weakest Side

Bobby + Goody. 2v1. All game.

Their right side is the weakest flank on the pitch. Our left side is built to attack it. Bobby on the wing plus Goody pushed high from LB, that is a permanent 2v1 against their right back. We attack it first. We attack it repeatedly.

The pattern
Bobby drives. Goody overlaps.
  • Build through the middle or attract them to our right side first
  • Quick ball into Bobby wide left
  • Bobby drives at their right back, body angled inside, ball at feet
  • Goody is already starting the overlap run on the outside
  • Their right back faces a 2v1, he cannot deal with both
  • If RB stays tight on Bobby → Goody is free outside for the cross
  • If RB goes to Goody → Bobby drives inside for the shot or cutback
The rule
Do not switch early.

Let the centre or right side attract their defenders first. Then deliver to Bobby. A pass to an unattended Bobby in a 2v1 is much more dangerous than a pass to a Bobby they have already organised against.

Their right back will get tired of this. He will start cheating across. That is the trigger for Pattern B.

08 Attacking Patterns

Four patterns. Four triggers.

Patterns are not plays we run blindly. Each is triggered by a specific picture, the press committing, the right side overloaded, a turnover, a marker following too tight. Read the trigger. Run the pattern.

Pattern A · Primary

Bait and Release

TriggerTheir press commits to Gabi.
  1. Destiny plays to Gabi. Gabi holds the ball, baits the press.
  2. Their pressing midfielder commits forward.
  3. Ibiso drops between the lines, before the pass, not after.
  4. Gabi plays Ibiso through the press.
  5. Ibiso turns and plays Rex/Fortune in the half-spaces.
  6. Bobby is already running behind, or Goody is the long diagonal option.
Critical:Ibiso's drop must happen before the ball moves, not after. Every build-up starts here.
Pattern B

Left to Right Switch

TriggerTheir RB and cover have collapsed onto Bobby.
  1. Bobby receives wide left, attracts their RB plus the cover.
  2. Bobby lays back to Ibiso (or Reuben).
  3. Rex is already waiting in the right half-space, demanding the switch.
  4. Quick diagonal switch from Ibiso to Rex.
  5. Rex plays Jobi into the space behind their isolated left back.
  6. Rex follows his pass into the box for the cutback.
Timing:Rex must wait until their LB is occupied with something else. A switch to an organised right side is wasted.
Pattern C

Counter Launch

TriggerWe win the ball + their line is high + Bobby or Jobi is already running.
  1. We win the ball anywhere in our half.
  2. First look: is Bobby or Jobi already running?
  3. If yes, immediate long ball over their high line.
  4. If no, Ibiso holds, team advances 10 metres, then attack.
  5. Whoever receives finishes alone or squares for the trailing runner.
Pre-planned:Every time we win the ball, Bobby and Jobi should already be looking to run. This is not reactive, it is the default.
Pattern D

Fortune Bounce

TriggerFortune receives with a marker tight on him.
  1. Fortune receives between lines, marker tight, back to goal.
  2. One-touch bounce off Ibiso/Reuben, the give-and-go.
  3. Fortune immediately moves into a new position, facing forward.
  4. Ibiso/Reuben delivers to Fortune's new position.
  5. Fortune drives at their disorganised midfield.
Why it works:Their ball-playing midfielders are not natural ball-winners. Fortune carrying forward after the bounce is a dangerous situation for them.
09 How We Defend

Mid-block. Patient. No chasing.

Cyper Reapers are a possession team. They want us to chase. We don't. We sit in the 4-4-2 mid-block, stay compact, force them wide, and wait. Their patient possession breaks down if we stay organised. Their press triggers are gifts.

DEFENCE 01
The 4-4-2 mid-block.
Sit 15-20m from our box. ≤10m vertical gap between the back four and the midfield four. Move as one unit. Always.
DEFENCE 02
Pressing their back 3.
In possession they build with a back 3. Fortune and Rex press the two high CBs. The dropped wide player is Bobby's or Jobi's man. Don't press without a trigger.
DEFENCE 03
Press triggers.
CB receives facing his own goal → Fortune and Rex sprint. Ball goes back to their GK → press immediately. Otherwise, hold the block.
Handling their dangerous LW (inside 10) - Gadus
The one player who can hurt us.

Their left winger plays like an inside 10. He drifts central, finds pockets, and turns to face goal. We cannot let that happen.

The assignment is simple:

  • In midfield zones (centrally) → Reuben tracks him.
  • In wide areas (our right flank) → Etienne tracks him.

The handoff is clear. Reuben does not chase him into wide zones. Etienne does not get pulled inside. The rule: he does not receive and turn facing our goal. Ever.

When they press high
This is opportunity, not threat.

Their press is the moment we manufacture an artificial transition. Do not panic.

  • Stay calm. Do not kick it long in panic.
  • Gabi or Kennedy holds, baits the presser to fully commit.
  • Ibiso drops, receives between the lines.
  • Play forward immediately.
  • If Ibiso is also closed, Reuben is the safety valve. Reset.

What we do NOT do: hoof it long because we are nervous. A long ball without a runner ready is possession given away. Patient build-up destroys their press better than panic.

10 Defensive Set Pieces

No ball-watching. Clear it.

A 6-2 winning team has set-piece quality. Stay disciplined. Mark your man. Clear your lines. We win the second balls.

SP 01
Gabi → their biggest aerial threat.
Their tallest, most dangerous header. Gabi is on him from before the ball is whipped in.
SP 02
Kennedy → their second aerial threat.
Second-most dangerous header. Kennedy doesn't lose him.
SP 03
Reuben → second balls.
Edge of the box. Anything cleared half-heartedly, Reuben wins it back and starts our counter.
11 Energy Plan

The 70th minute is ours.

The team that conserves energy through minutes 1-60 runs the other team ragged in minutes 60-90. This is not optional, it is part of the game plan. Smart with our sprints. Patient in the block. The last 30 minutes are won in the first 60.

Sprint here

Full commitment. No holding back.

  • Runs in behind their high line, Bobby and Jobi
  • Press triggers when activated, GK back-pass, CB facing own goal
  • The moment after Ibiso plays forward, Goody, Rex, Fortune attacking the artificial transition
  • Recovery runs after we lose the ball in their half
Do not sprint here

They want us to chase. Don't.

  • Chasing their ball-playing midfielders around our mid-block
  • Walking the ball into our shape after a clearance, jog with purpose
  • Sprinting back into position when we have time, walk, breathe, reset
  • Closing down their CBs when we are not on a press trigger
HABIT 01
Use every stoppage.
Throw-ins, goal kicks, fouls, substitutions, these are recovery seconds. Hydrate. Breathe. Talk. Reset shape. Take them.
HABIT 02
Mid-block is energy-efficient.
Let them carry the ball into our shape. We do not need to win it in their half, we need to win it in ours and break.
HABIT 03
Pace the runs in behind.
Bobby and Jobi look to run every transition, but the long sprints are picked moments, not every touch. Pick the ball. Pick the moment. Commit.

14 Summary

Four battles. Win them. Win the game.

If you remember nothing else from this document, remember these four. They are what decides this match. Win these four battles, we win the game.

Battle 01
We bait the press successfully.Play through their press 3-4 times in the first half and their confidence drops. They drop off. That gives us more space.
Battle 02
We attack the space behind their high line.Bobby and Jobi commit to those runs every time we win the ball deep. One goal from in behind changes the entire game.
Battle 03
Their LW does not turn in dangerous positions.Reuben in midfield, Etienne when wide. He can have the ball with his back to goal, he cannot turn.
Battle 04
We win the energy battle.Smart with our sprints. Patient in the mid-block. The last 30 minutes are ours if we manage the first 60.
